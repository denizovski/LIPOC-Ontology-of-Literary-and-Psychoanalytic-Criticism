LIPOC

Ontology of Literary and Psychoanalytic Criticism

Digital Humanities and Digital Knowledge · Bologna, BO · Italy ·

This project belongs to the 'Knowledge Representation and Extraction' course of University of Bologna, a.y. 2018/2019.

The purpose of the project is to extract information and represent knowledge of literary and psychoanalytic criticism on two examples of fantasy fiction genre, Kafka's Novella The Metamorphosis and Robert Louis Stevenson's short novel Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde, in LOD environment.

Use Case

This project aims to analyse the given literal text by basing on some computational tools and the computational results are processed and then represented in the knowledge graph which illustrates the relationship between the characters, the themes of the literal texts and Freud's psychoanalysis(id, ego, superego).

LIPOC will be applied to two different texts.

Franz Kafka

The Metamorphosis

Robert Louis Stevenson

Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde

The methodology

Starting from the articles written by scholars, the texts are being analysed also with computational tools. The idea is to show the relationship among the protagonist's physical change(metamorphosis, transformation) throughout the story and their social relations which are represented with the literal themes(change, alienation) and the characters' connections with Freud's psychoanalysis(id, ego, superego).

Linguistic Analysis

Psychoanalysis

Pyschoanalysis in literature can be done with different methods; the method applied in the ontology is the method of showing which "when the author puts himself outside the story by providing the opportunity for figures to show their characterization through dialogue and action (Pickering and Hoeper, 1981: 27)".

By using an online tool for Regular Expression, the dialogues in quotes are extracted to tag each character with id, ego or superego.

The dialogue analysis of Dr.Jekyll and Mr.Hyde shows that Utterson's dialogues are mostly rational and formal which illustrates the moral values; from Dr.Jekyll's dialogues it can be understood that he is following his instinct or he is tempted; on the other hand, Mr.Hyde doesn't have as many conversation as Utterson and Dr. Jekyll have but his name is mentioned very often during the conversation of others, his dialogues are direct, short and in a rude way.

Ontology Development

By taking into account all the knowledge that has been extracted via different tools, an ontology (LIPOC) has been designed to represent it. LIPOC will represent basic information about text, its language usage and critical analysis and psychoanalysis by starting from the whole composition and going deeper in hidden meanings and interpretations of the extracted sentences. Psychoanalysis

Psychoanalysis

Freud's psyhcoanalytic theories says that the unconscious mind includes three main components: the id, the ego, and the superego. The detection of id, ego and superego is done by extracting the dialogues that the characters involve. With the extracted dialogues, the sentiment analysis is done and the characters are assigned to one of those components.

Id is related with pleasure; it is the unconscious force that drives us toward our intrinsic needs, importantly sexual and aggressive, but also our wants and desires.

Superego is our mind’s sense of morality, typically learned from our parents. It urges us to go about satisfying our needs in a manner that is socially acceptable.

Ego becomes the balance between the instinct of the id and the morality of the superego. While the id wants to fulfill its needs immediately without hesitation, and the superego wants to uphold social and cultural norms, the ego acts as the referee between the two and helps us satisfy our needs while still acting socially and morally appropriate. It is the one that enters everyday reality; the behavior that we exhibit to the world is the result of the ego’s moderation of the id and the superego.
